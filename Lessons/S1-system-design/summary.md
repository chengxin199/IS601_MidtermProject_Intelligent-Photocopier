## üìö Key Concepts Mastered
1. **System Architecture**: Understanding the various architectural patterns (e.g., microservices, monolithic, serverless) and when to use each based on project requirements.
2. **Scalability**: Techniques for designing systems that can handle increased load, including horizontal vs. vertical scaling.
3. **Reliability and Fault Tolerance**: Key strategies to ensure system uptime, including redundancy, failover mechanisms, and load balancing.
4. **Data Management**: Understanding data storage solutions (SQL vs. NoSQL) and their impact on system performance and scalability.
5. **API Design**: Best practices for designing RESTful and GraphQL APIs, focusing on usability and performance.
6. **Monitoring and Logging**: Setting up effective monitoring and logging systems to track performance and diagnose issues.

## üîç Common Pitfalls Avoided
- **Overcomplicating Design**: Avoiding unnecessary complexity in system architecture that can lead to maintenance headaches.
- **Neglecting Non-Functional Requirements**: Recognizing the importance of performance, security, and usability in system design.
- **Ignoring Documentation**: Ensuring all components and APIs are well-documented to facilitate team collaboration and future development.
- **Underestimating Testing**: Prioritizing thorough testing (unit, integration, and load testing) to catch issues early in the development cycle.

- **Deployment Frequency**: Increased deployment frequency with CI/CD practices, leading to faster delivery of features.
- **User Satisfaction**: Improved user feedback ratings through enhanced usability and performance of applications.
