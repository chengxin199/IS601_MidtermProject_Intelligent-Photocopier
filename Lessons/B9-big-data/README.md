### Learning Path
1. **Introduction to Big Data**: Start with understanding what big data is and why it's crucial in today's data-driven world.
2. **Explore Characteristics**: Dive deep into the defining characteristics of big data.
3. **Get Hands-On with Tools**: Familiarize yourself with Hadoop, Spark, and NoSQL databases through practical exercises.
4. **Data Processing Techniques**: Learn the differences between batch and stream processing.
5. **Study Real-World Applications**: Analyze case studies that demonstrate the impact of big data.
6. **Build a Project**: Apply your knowledge by developing your own big data project.

### Assessment Criteria
- Completion of quizzes after each module to assess understanding.
- Quality and functionality of the final project, demonstrating the application of learned techniques.
- Participation in discussions and peer reviews.

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Skipping foundational concepts; ensure you grasp the basics before moving on.
- Underestimating the importance of practical application; hands-on practice is critical.
- Failing to document your project; clear documentation is key for others to understand your work.

### Reflection Questions
- What are the most significant benefits of using big data in industry?
- How can big data influence decision-making processes in organizations?
- What challenges do you foresee in working with big data technologies?
